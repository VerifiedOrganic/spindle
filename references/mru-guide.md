# MRU guide

This reference covers motivation-reaction units for line-level scene control.
Use it when prose feels out of order, emotionally vague, or mechanically flat.

## Core pattern

An MRU works best when outside stimulus lands before the internal and external
response it causes.

1. Present the motivating stimulus.
2. Show the involuntary reaction.
3. Show the deliberate response.

## Typical order

Use this order unless you have a clear reason to break it.

- Stimulus: what the POV character perceives.
- Feeling: the immediate body-level response.
- Reflex: the instinctive movement or thought.
- Rational action: the chosen next move.
- Speech: what the character says after processing the beat.

## Fast example

Bad order:

`Mira ducked. The arrow cracked past her ear.`

Better order:

`The arrow cracked past Mira's ear. Her shoulders jerked tight. She dropped
behind the parapet and swore.`

## When to break the pattern

Break the clean sequence only when doing so creates a specific effect.

- Use delayed stimulus to hide information briefly.
- Use clipped reaction beats in combat for speed.
- Use distorted order when the POV is dissociated, drunk, exhausted, or under
  magic.

If you break the order, make the intention legible on the page.
